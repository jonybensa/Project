Port Olimpic

This is the place to be in summer! Here a string of upmarket lounge bars, such as CDLC, Shoko and Bestial attract a well-heeled cosmopolitan crowd to sip cocktails on their beach terraces before everyone moves indoors to go to the dancefloor.

Most of these places close at 3am unfortunately but the infamous Opium, plus Pacha and Catwalk keep going until 6am - of these Opium has the strongest claim to be the best club in the city, and is reliably packed.

El Born (a hidden jewel)

If you are looking for even more sophisticated place to experience the night life, you must visit El Born. You will find yourself surrounded with cafes and bars and all sorts of trendy attires. Miramelindo is one of such places, you will find all sorts of cocktails, but cava cocktail is a specialty here. Probe the backstreets further and you will find surprises such as Cat Bar in the hidden spots.

Las Ramblas

Most probably you will start from Las Ramblas if you are a newbie and you will find narrow streets just like a medieval old town. It is considered to be the busiest place in the daytime while by the time sun sets down, the streets will be crowded with artists giving their performances. That's time when tourist will start heading towards bars and clubs for drinks and dinner. The Kiosk La Cazalla and Boulevard Culture Club are few of the renowned places in Las Ramblas. When move down the street, you will find a proper irish setting, Wild Rover Irish Pub, in Barcelona. When you are looking for a more classy spot, go to Le Pop Cocktail Bar. You will be amazed by the creative cocktails and sparkling aperitivos this bar has to offer. The bar claims to have the best bartenders in the town which is undoubtedly true.

Raval District (for techno lovers)

Raval is on the other side of the Las Ramblas where the streets are full with kids playing cricket or football. At the heart of this district, there hip bars such as Ambar which is continually filled with kids, lovely couples and lost souls. For a more glamourous crowd there is a cocktail bar nearby Marmalade. You can head to Nou de la Rambia to explore clubs such as Les Enfants (a Spanish disco), Moog (genuine electronic music/DJs) and Sala Apolo (small venue with Stereophonics and mid weeks parties like Nasty Mondays).
For a more relaxed time, get into Shenanigans Irish Pub for enjoying live music (mostly on Friday and Saturday) and pints. Hanging out with your friends in this authentic district can be even more fun when you visit Hipster's Bar Crawl. This will go miles to show you how the real night looks like.

Gracia

This district has got the same vibe as Raval and in fact locally dominated. Here you can find the real Catalan culture and aspiring artists with less foreigners. There are some exquisite bars located in this area such as La Cigale (known for good mojitos, Tapas and Cocktails) and La Fourmi (a great neighborhood spot with very good food and service).
